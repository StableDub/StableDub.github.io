Abstract

The visual dubbing task aims to generate mouth movements synchronized with the driving audio, which has seen significant progress in recent years. However, previous methods struggle to strike an appropriate balance between visual quality, audio-lip synchronization, and generalization, failing to meet the demands for high-quality generation and diverse scenarios in applications. In this paper, we propose StableDub, a simple yet effective method for high-quality and generalizable visual dubbing. Specifically, we employ a dual-branch Mamba-Transformer U-Nets framework to ensure image quality and generalization. We attain video temporal consistency by incorporating a union facial mask and a memory-efficient temporal Mamba layer. Moreover, we propose a latent coarse facial loss to ensure lip synchronization. Extensive experimental results demonstrate that StableDub outperforms the state-of-the-art methods in various aspects.

Method Overview

Given an appearance image, an input video and the driving audio feature, StableDub aims to generate the audio-lip synced video by inpainting the lower-half face of the input video. StableDub consists of dual U-Nets with similar structures, each composed of several Mamba-Transformer layers. The appearance net ensures the preservation of the portrait identity, while the denoising net aligns lip movements with audio and ensures video consistency. We specially derive the union facial mask and coarse facial mask from the fine facial mask to assist in the optimization of temporal consistency and lip sync.
